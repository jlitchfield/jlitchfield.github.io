Solstice Initiative

The age of solar has arrived.

Solstice is the first provider of community-based solar power to any American that cannot install a rooftop array. Households can sign up to participate in a shared solar array and save money by switching to solar — no upfront cost, no rooftop, and no installation required.

Pain Points

Community shared solar is the first solution for renters, but also entails higher customer turnover versus the more permanent assets of rooftop solar.

Without a visible solar array on the roof, customers lack the tangibility of solar benefits that traditional clients have. Solstice panels are out-of-sight, therefore out-of-mind.

Painless Account Creation

Users are able to quickly scan their energy bill to painlessly create an account. Furthermore, users may log in with their Facebook credentials, which can later be used for social sharing with the community.

A Sense of Community

The News Feed, a core part of the experience, educates users about the advantages of community solar. Users can also learn about community events.

Creating Stickiness

Badges have been created to enable users respond to opportunities of winning and collecting awards that in turn can be displayed to other community members in order to increase engagement.

The Dashboard

The Dashboard allows users to quickly see how much money they are saving each month. Also, users may see historical data to see how well they’re performing overtime. Finally, users can see how they perform against the community, further driving engagement.

Ambient Orb for the Home

Solstice Initiative clients receive a complimentary Ambient Orb for the home. The orb provides visual feedback to quickly see how much energy they are saving based on historical use. If the client is making progress the orb glows green. Conversely, the orb is red the client is not performing well.
