I am a huge geek.

As a young boy my favorite activities included drawing, painting, and reading. I also wore my socks to my knees! I also loved computers — and thanks to my best friend Jon —, the Mac. I loved the Mac before it was cool. My first computer was a Commodore 64. I started to teach myself BASIC. In 1997, at the age of 17, instead of buying a car like my friends I bought a lime green iMac.

In high school, I started to study graphic design. I haven't looked back since. I earned my BFA in Graphic Design from the University of Massachusetts at Lowell.

As an adult my favorite activities still include drawing (in Illustrator), painting (in Photoshop), and reading anything I can related to design, process, web standards, front-end development, and the like.

When I am not “geeking” out to design and code you can find me in a number of places. I might be hosting a 500+ person pub crawl for my non-profit, the Mike McNeil Organization. We've had thousands of crawlers and raised over $50,000 for cancer research.

In my free time, I like to try out new restaurants. I also love coffee houses and museums. I love front porches and cards with family and friends. These are some of the favorite times.
